Pharmacodynamic effects


Pharmacodynamics is the study of how a dug affects an organism.

Pharmocodynamic effects are of two types. They are:

Desirable effects

This means that the drug is working, and it is beneficial to the patient. In short, it is the desired outcome.


Undesirable effects

Undesirable effects or Adverse drug reactions can be further divided into two categories. They are:

  • Expected/Type A – Adverse Drug Reactions
  • Unexpected/Type B – Adverse Drug Reactions

Expected/Type A - Adverse Drug Reactions – These are the adverse drug reactions that you expect while taking a drug. They can include drowsiness, headaches, dizziness to name just a few.

Unexpected/Type B – Adverse Drug Reactions - These are the adverse drug reactions that you will not expect while taking a drug. These can include organ injuries, risk situations, late effects carcinogenesis and many more.
