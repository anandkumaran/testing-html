Rawlin and Thomson Classification


One of the simplest forms of classification is the Rawlin and Thomas Classification which was devised in 19991. In this, Adverse Drug Reactions are divided into Type A, Type B and Others which include Type C, D and E.

Type A: The reactions will usually be dose related. They are predictable, acute and related to mechanism of action. The risk factor of Type A is less than that of Type B.

Type B: the reactions will be independent of the dose administered to the patient. It could be due to an allergy or hypersensitivity. They will be Idiosyncratic, unpredictable, acute/sub-acute.

Type C: These reactions are due to chronic or continuous use of drugs.

Type D: These reactions refer to carcinogenic and teratogenic effects. These reactions are delayed in onset and are very rare since extensive studies are done on the side effects of a drug before it is licensed.

Type E: These reactions occur when a drug is stopped or if the dosage is reduced. This can include withdrawal reaction or second malignancies following successful chemotherapy.
