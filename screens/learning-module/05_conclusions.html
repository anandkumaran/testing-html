Conclusions

  • Compassionate care is a skilled interpersonal process that is important for staff, patients and families.
  • There is much knowledge about the things that are important to patients in relation to good caring, but less about what practices promote and support compassion in the workplace.
  • Knowing who people are and what matters to them, understanding how they feel, and working with them to achieve compassionate relationship-centred care that has meaning for both those who give and receive care, is integral to providing compassionate care.
  • Developing relationships with self, patients and families, colleagues and the wider organisation are essential to achieve the quality ambitions at the forefront of policy, and professional and practice-related aspirations about compassion.
