Perform or perish model Relational and responsive model
Pace: quick fix, short term, process driven, pushing and fixing. Complexity: longer term, focuses on people and perceptions, brokering.
External: top-down agenda, local context is largely overlooked, ‘off-the-shelf’, ‘one-size-fits-all’ approaches are applied. Locally contextual factors are fully acknowledged and addressed, solutions are tailored to the situation, existing models are modified accordingly.
A select few determine goals and the direction of change. All groups, including service users/carers, are involved in deciding goals and direction of change.
Punitive and transactional leadership from the top, little unit level leadership. Empowering, inspiring and transformational leadership style at all levels, especially at the unit level.
Metrics matter: superficial, often quantitative targets for success, for example patient flow. Meaning matters: relational, dynamic qualitative indicators of success, peoples’ experiences.
Scored. Profiled.
Impoverished change environment results, and the ‘senses’ are reduced. Enriched change environment results, and the ‘senses’ are enhanced.
(Patterson et al 2011)
