Action plan for developing a compassionate organisation Suggested activity
Declaring compassion as a core value.
  • Maintain a consistent message of compassion across all activity in the organisation, for example using compassion as the foundation for all policies.
  • Develop organisational language that supports compassion, for example rather than talking about behaviours that are expected, talk about behaviours that individuals value and aspire to.
  • Develop guidance for good practice in communicating in a compassionate way, for example notices about visiting. Rather than writing in red and bold font saying that visiting is strictly between certain times and restricted to two people to a patient, this can be communicated in a way that conveys the preferred practice, but that invites people to discuss other options.
Rewarding rather than punishing compassionate care.
  • Gather stories of excellent compassionate care-giving and share them in a range of ways, for example during meetings at all levels across the organisation.
  • Support people in noticing compassionate acts, and give real-time feedback, for example cards that say ‘caught in the act of caring’.
Honing compassionate and relationship skills.
  • Develop communication based on the process of inquiring appreciatively.
  • Ask questions such as ‘What have we got right for you?’ and ‘How can we make your experience even better?’.
  • Seek feedback about interpersonal skills.
  • Explore the experience of caring from the perspectives of patients, families and staff in meaningful ways. Ensure this is part of routine activity.
Creating a safe place for meaningful conversation in the workplace.
  • Hold short daily meetings in the workplace to explore what worked well and what could have been done better, and give positive feedback.
  • Create realistic models for peer support in the workplace. For example, monthly sessions at ward level to support staff to share their experiences of caring.
Challenging models of professionalism.
  • Perform an extensive listening exercise to find out what matters to staff in the context of their work.
  • Make explicit the core values and behaviours of the organisation to enable staff to challenge practices that do not meet these aspirations.
Defining compassion as a management and leadership competence skill.
  • Integrate compassion into existing performance management frameworks and develop capacity for compassion.
  • Audit interpersonal skills to provide regular feedback and support development.
